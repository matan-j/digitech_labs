# age-adaptation.md — Grade-Level Content Calibration

Apply these rules whenever generating content for a specific audience band.

---

## AUDIENCE BAND DEFINITIONS

| Band          | Grades     | Ages      |
|---------------|------------|-----------|
| Elementary    | 4–6        | 9–12      |
| Middle School | 7–9        | 12–15     |
| High School   | 10–12      | 15–18     |

---

## ELEMENTARY SCHOOL (grades 4–6)

### Language
- Short sentences
- Simple vocabulary
- One concept per sentence
- Avoid abstract nouns unless immediately illustrated

### Examples
- From daily life: food, games, school, family, sports
- Concrete and familiar
- Visual or physical analogies preferred

### Tasks
- Max 10–12 minutes per activity
- Step-by-step guided (not open-ended)
- Clear success criteria ("You're done when you have 3 answers")
- Scaffolded worksheets with visual cues

### Theory
- Max 8–10 minutes at once
- Always followed by an immediate do-something step
- Use "I do → we do → you do" structure

### Output
- Drawing, labeling, filling in, matching
- Short written answers (2–4 sentences)
- Physical products: cards, posters, sticky notes

---

## MIDDLE SCHOOL (grades 7–9)

### Language
- Clear and precise
- Can handle more domain-specific vocabulary if introduced and explained
- Some abstraction is fine if anchored in an example first

### Examples
- Mix of personal life and broader world
- Technology, social media, real companies, current events
- Can work with real data and real tools

### Tasks
- 12–18 minutes per activity
- Some open-ended tasks
- Partner or small group work is highly effective
- Can manage multi-step tasks with partial guidance

### Theory
- Max 12–15 minutes at once
- Connect to "why does this affect ME or my world?"
- Include discussion prompts between theory blocks

### Output
- Mini-analysis, short reports, annotated charts
- Peer presentations (1–2 minutes)
- Digital artifacts (Google Sheet, Flourish chart, Canva design)

---

## HIGH SCHOOL (grades 10–12)

### Language
- Full professional or academic register where appropriate
- Domain terminology expected and can be used with precision
- More nuanced framing ("tradeoffs," "implications," "limitations")

### Examples
- Real-world professional and civic contexts
- Industry data, policy decisions, business cases
- Global and local examples both valid

### Tasks
- 15–25 minutes per activity
- More autonomy and independent decision-making
- Debate, argumentation, and analysis valued
- Can produce original work from a brief without heavy scaffolding

### Theory
- Up to 15 minutes, but must be connected to a problem or case
- Students expected to question and analyze, not just absorb
- Critical framing: "What's wrong with this approach?"

### Output
- Structured written analysis
- Designed infographic or visual presentation
- Policy memo or recommendation
- Data dashboard or annotated visualization
- Short research brief

---

## CALIBRATION CHECKLIST

Before finalizing any output, verify:

- [ ] Vocabulary is appropriate for the band
- [ ] Task length matches cognitive stamina
- [ ] Examples are relevant and recognizable
- [ ] Theory blocks are within time limits
- [ ] Output format is achievable without frustration
- [ ] Instructions are clear without being condescending
- [ ] Challenge level is high enough to be interesting
- [ ] Scaffolding is high enough to ensure success

---

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

| Mistake                                  | Fix                                               |
|------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Using adult vocabulary for elementary    | Replace with concrete words + visuals             |
| Open-ended tasks for young learners      | Add specific steps and constraints                |
| Simple tasks for high school             | Add analytical layer, "what would you recommend?" |
| Same examples across age bands           | Adapt examples to the social world of the learner |
| Long reading passages for any age        | Break into chunks, add visual anchors             |
| One-size worksheet                       | Design band-specific scaffolding levels           |
